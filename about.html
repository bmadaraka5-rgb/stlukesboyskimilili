Our History

Founded in 1979, St. Luke's Boys High School has grown from a small community initiative into one of Kenya's leading boys' boarding schools, consistently producing top national performers.

1979
Foundation

Established by the Catholic Diocese of Bungoma with 45 pioneer students and 3 teachers.

1985
First KCSE Class

Produced first KCSE candidates with 80% qualifying for university.

1998
EXTRA COUNTY School Status

Awarded extra county School status by the Ministry of Education.

2015
ICT Integration

Launched 1:1 device program and digital learning platform.

2024
KCSE Excellence

Achieved mean score of 9.2 (A-), ranking top 10 nationally.
